Introduction

India is significantly affected by climate change, leading to diverse impacts on its environment, economy, and society.

Temperature Rise

India has experienced a steady increase in average temperatures, leading to heatwaves and altering weather patterns.

According to data from the Indian Meteorological Department, average temperatures across India have risen by about 0.7Â°C over the last century, with more significant warming observed in recent decades.

Extreme Events

Increased frequency and intensity of cyclones, floods, and droughts are becoming more common due to climate change.

For instance, cyclones like Cyclone Amphan and Cyclone Nisarga have caused extensive damage in coastal regions, highlighting the vulnerability of these areas to extreme weather events exacerbated by climate change.

Water Scarcity

Changing rainfall patterns and melting glaciers threaten India's water security, affecting agriculture and urban populations.

Regions like Rajasthan and parts of Maharashtra face severe water scarcity issues, exacerbated by declining groundwater levels and erratic monsoon patterns influenced by climate change.

Interesting Facts

  • India is one of the most vulnerable countries to climate change impacts due to its large population and dependence on agriculture.
  • The Himalayan glaciers, a crucial water source for rivers like the Ganges and Indus, are melting at an alarming rate.
  • Indian cities like Delhi and Kolkata face increasing air pollution exacerbated by climate change effects.
  • Rising sea levels threaten coastal areas, including major cities like Mumbai and Chennai.
  • The Sundarbans, home to endangered species like the Bengal tiger, is at risk from rising sea levels and extreme weather.
